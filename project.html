The Atacama Desert is an astonishing place, one of the driest landscapes on Earth, with almost continuous year-round cloudless nights during which we can observe the vast Milky Way. A prime space for astronomical and biodiversity studies. Every three to ten years, it experiences a super bloom of flowers called Desierto Florido , when it receives just enough water to magnificently bloom into an array of colors, attracting rare insects and climate-resilient microorganisms that might help us decipher things about our past and future on this planet.

Despite these unique characteristics and its enormous ecological importance, it has also been a site of intensive lithium and copper extraction, while more recently becoming, as the media has dubbed it, the world’s largest “garment graveyard.”
